The Castles of Burgundy

Game Board




Tip: Click on areas of the game board to find out what they do!

Victory Point (VP) Track

Tracks your points using your playing piece. Once you reach 100 points, place your VP tile 100 side face-up on your board next to the used die space. For 200 points, the VP tile is flipped to show the 200 side face-up.

Phase Goods

Place the goods that will be used for each phase here.

Phase Letter

Game Phases are from A-E.

Phase Bonus

The amount of Victory Points (VP) you will earn when you complete a a colored region. You get 10 VP for Phase A, 8 VP for Phase B... and 2 VP for Phase E.

Round Spaces

At the start of a Phase, place the goods on the Phase Goods space here. Rounds are indicated by the number of empty spaces on the Round Spaces. 1 empty space is Round 1, 2 empty spaces is Round 2... 5 empty spaces is Round 5.

Central Black Depot

Every round during your turn, you can buy a tile from this depot for 2 silver.

Bonus Tile: Castles

The first player to build on all their castle (dark green) regions gets the large Victory tile, and the second player to do so as well gets the remaining tile.

Bonus Tile: Building

The first player to build on all their city (brown) regions gets the large Victory tile, and the second player to do so as well gets the remaining tile.

Bonus Tile: Ships

The first player to build on all their ship (blue) regions gets the large Victory tile, and the second player to do so as well gets the remaining tile.

Bonus Tile: Knowledge

The first player to build on all their knowledge (yellow) regions gets the large Victory tile, and the second player to do so as well gets the remaining tile.

Bonus Tile: Mines

The first player to build on all their mine (grey) regions gets the large Victory tile, and the second player to do so as well gets the remaining tile.

Bonus Tile: Pastures

The first player to build on all their pasture (green) regions gets the large Victory tile, and the second player to do so as well gets the remaining tile.

Turn Order Track

The farther your piece on this track, the earlier your turn. For stacked pieces, the earlier player is always on top.

Numbered Depot 1

The large square space is for the goods for that round while the hexagonal numbered spaces determine the tile to be placed based on color and based on the number of players.

Numbered Depot 2

The large square space is for the goods for that round while the hexagonal numbered spaces determine the tile to be placed based on color and based on the number of players.

Numbered Depot 3

The large square space is for the goods for that round while the hexagonal numbered spaces determine the tile to be placed based on color and based on the number of players.

Numbered Depot 4

The large square space is for the goods for that round while the hexagonal numbered spaces determine the tile to be placed based on color and based on the number of players.

Numbered Depot 5

The large square space is for the goods for that round while the hexagonal numbered spaces determine the tile to be placed based on color and based on the number of players.

Numbered Depot 6

The large square space is for the goods for that round while the hexagonal numbered spaces determine the tile to be placed based on color and based on the number of players.

Numbered Depot 6: Special Space

This special tile space is used for a 3-Player game, where for Phases A-C-E, a castle is placed here, but for Phases B & D, a mine is placed instead.

Player Board


Tip: Click on areas of the player board to find out what they do!



Tip: Click on areas of the player board to find out what they do!

Used Die Storage

Place your Used Action Dice here.

Victory Point (VP) Table

Use this table to determine how many points you receive based on the size of the colored region you completed. 1 pt = size 1 region, 3 pts = size 2 region... 36 pts = size 8 region.

Worker Tile Storage

Place your Worker Tiles here.

Silverling Storage

Place your Silverlings here.

Sold Goods Storage

Place your sold goods here.

Goods Storage

You can place three (3) types (color) of goods here. (e.g. You place a green good, two (2) yellow goods, and a grey good in a separate space for each type (color).

Hex Tiles Storage/Build Queue

You place the tiles you get from the game board here. If it's full, you have to discard one before placing the new tile in this space

Dice Action Summary

Top-Bottom: (1) Claim a tile with one die, (2) Place a tile from your storage to your estate, (3) Sell goods according to number of dice, (4) Claim 2 workers

Extra Buy Action

Once every turn, for 2 silverlings, you can buy from the central black depot.

Estate Number

This indicates which player board you're using. Estate 1 is the default and simplest estate while the rest are advanced versions.

Knowledge Tile Summary

Knowledge tiles provide a number of bonuses. Click here for more info.

Ships Tile Summary

Ships move you forward on the turn order track and enables you to get all the goods from a numbered depot of your choice.

Animals Tile Summary

Animals give you Victory Points (VP). The amount of VP is shown on the tile plus you gain the amount of VP from animals of the same type already on your pasture.

Castles Tile Summary

Castles give you an extra Dice action, regardless of dice value.

Warehouse Tile Summary

Warehouses let you sell a Goods type of your choice from your Goods Storage.

Carpenter's Workshop Tile Summary

The Carpenter's Workshop lets you claim a building (brown) tile of your choice from the Game Board.

Church Tile Summary

A Church lets you claim either a Castle (dark green), Mine (grey), or a Knowledge (yellow) tile of your choice from the Game Board.

Market Tile Summary

A Market lets you claim either a Ship (blue) or an Animal (green) tile of your choice from the Game Board.

Boarding House Tile Summary

The Boarding House gives you four (4) worker tiles.

Bank Tile Summary

The Bank gives you two (2) Silverlings.

City Hall Tile Summary

The City Hall lets you place a Hex Tile from your storage to your estate, regardless of number (with respect to the adjacency rule).

Watchtower Tile Summary

The Watchtower gives you four (4) Victory Points (VP).

Mine Tile Summary

For each Mine you have at the start of every Phase, gain a Silverling.

Region: City

You can place your Building (brown) Tiles here. Note that you can only place one (1) Building per Building type in each City Region (e.g. Your Size 6 City Region has a watchtower, therefore you cannot place another watchtower here).

Region: Castle

You can place your Castle (dark green) Tiles here.

Region: Ship

You can place your Ship (blue) Tiles here.

Region: Pastures

You can place our Animal (green) Tiles here.

Region: Knowledge

You can place your Knowledge (yellow) Tiles here.

Region: Mines

You can place your Mine (grey) Tiles here.
